Pull

1.Start by selecting an appropriate pair of dumbbells with a weight that suits your fitness level. You can stand or sit on a bench with a backrest for support.
2.Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, keeping your back straight and your core engaged for stability.
3.Hold a dumbbell in each hand, allowing your arms to fully extend down by your sides, with your palms facing forward (a supinated grip).
4.Keep your upper arms close to your torso, and your elbows should be fully extended at the start of the movement.
5.Begin the exercise by flexing your elbow joints while keeping your upper arms stationary. Curl the dumbbells upwards towards your shoulders in a controlled manner.
6.As you lift the dumbbells, exhale and focus on contracting your bicep muscles.
7.Continue to curl the dumbbells until they are close to your shoulder or your biceps are fully contracted. Your forearms should be perpendicular to the ground.
8.Hold the contracted position briefly to emphasize the squeeze on your biceps.
9.Slowly lower the dumbbells back to the starting position, inhaling as you do so. Keep the movement controlled and avoid swinging or using momentum.
10.Repeat the exercise for the desired number of repetitions.
